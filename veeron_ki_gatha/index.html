The Six Pillars

India's Gallantry Awards System

🥇
Wartime

1st Precedence

Param Vir Chakra

परम वीर चक्र · "Wheel of the Ultimate Brave"

India's supreme wartime honor. Awarded for the most conspicuous bravery in the presence of the enemy.

21 total · 14 posthumous · Designed by Savitri Khanolkar

🥈
Wartime

2nd Precedence

Maha Vir Chakra

महावीर चक्र · "Medal for Great Bravery"

The second-highest wartime decoration, for conspicuous gallantry in the presence of the enemy — on land, sea, or air.

213 total (including 2020 Galwan Valley clash)

🎖️
Wartime

3rd Precedence

Vir Chakra

वीर चक्र · "Wheel of the Brave"

The third-highest wartime gallantry award, for acts of bravery in the field of battle — close combat or leadership under fire.

Thousands awarded since 1947

The Bravest of the Brave

Kashmir War · 1947–48
Param Vir Chakra
Posthumous

Major Somnath Sharma

4 Kumaon Regiment · Badgam, Kashmir · Nov 3, 1947

The first-ever PVC awardee. With a fractured arm in a plaster cast, he held Badgam airfield against 700+ raiders.

Read Full Story ›

The Battle

On Nov 3, 1947, D Company of 4 Kumaon came under sudden attack by over 700 Pakistani raiders. Despite being ordered not to engage — and despite his plastered arm — Sharma chose to hold the airfield, buying critical time that saved Srinagar.

"The enemy is only 50 yards from us. We are heavily outnumbered. I shall not withdraw an inch but will fight to the last man and the last round."

Unsung Insight

The PVC was designed by Savitri Khanolkar — a Swiss-born woman who married an Indian officer. Major Sharma was her son-in-law. She never told anyone of this connection during the medal's design process.

Param Vir Chakra
Posthumous★ Often Overlooked

Naik Jadunath Singh

1 Rajput Regiment · Naushera, Kashmir · Feb 1948

Repulsed enemy attacks single-handedly. Found later — standing dead in his trench.

Read Full Story ›

Last Stand

Jadunath Singh led the defense of an isolated picquet at Naushera. When the third wave came, all his comrades were dead. He fixed his bayonet and charged alone into the massed raiders. His body was found standing against a sandbag wall — frozen in his last defiant stance.

Congo · 1961 — India's Only PVC Beyond Its Borders
Param Vir Chakra
Posthumous★ Only PVC Outside India

Capt. Gurbachan Singh Salaria

3/1 Gurkha Rifles · Elizabethville, Katanga, Congo · Dec 5, 1961

Charged a Katangese rebel roadblock alone to free trapped UN forces.

Read Full Story ›

The Mission

Salaria's platoon encountered a large rebel position. Under intense fire and flanked on multiple sides, Salaria charged the enemy himself, killing approximately 40 rebels with his kukri and sten gun before succumbing to a bullet in his throat.

Sino-Indian War · 1962
Param Vir Chakra
Posthumous

Major Shaitan Singh

13 Kumaon · Rezang La, Ladakh · Nov 18, 1962

Led 123 soldiers with WWII-era rifles against 3000 Chinese with automatic weapons at 18,000 ft.

Read Full Story ›

The Last Stand

114 of 123 died at their posts. The Chinese suffered over 1000 casualties. When Rezang La was revisited, dead Jawans were found in their trenches still holding their weapons — every single man with several bullet wounds.

Liberation War · 1971
Param Vir Chakra
Posthumous★ Only IAF PVC

Flying Officer Nirmal Jit Singh Sekhon

No. 18 Squadron IAF · Srinagar · Dec 14, 1971

The only Indian Air Force officer to be awarded the PVC. Took off during an air raid and engaged 6 Pakistani Sabres alone.

Dogfight over Srinagar

Despite his airfield being under attack, Sekhon took off in his Folland Gnat. Outnumbered 1 to 6, he shot down two Sabres before his own aircraft was hit. His supreme sacrifice saved Srinagar from heavy bombardment.

Param Vir Chakra
Posthumous

2nd Lt. Arun Khetarpal

17 Poona Horse · Battle of Basantar · Dec 16, 1971

The youngest recipient of the PVC (21 years old). Destroyed 4 enemy tanks before his own tank caught fire.

Read Full Story ›

The Supreme Sacrifice

Ordered to abandon his burning tank ("Famagusta"), Khetarpal replied over the radio: "No Sir, I will not abandon my tank. My main gun is still working and I will get these bastards." He destroyed the final enemy tank before succumbing to a direct hit.

Siachen / IPKF · 1987
Param Vir Chakra

Naib Subedar Bana Singh

8 JAK LI · Siachen Glacier · June 26, 1987

Scaled a 457m high vertical ice wall under heavy blizzard and cleared the impenetrable 'Quaid Post' at 21,000 feet.

Read Full Story ›

Operation Rajiv

Leading a team of five, Bana Singh approached the fortress-like Quaid Post (later renamed Bana Post) from an unexpected, near-vertical ice wall. Using grenades and bayonets in close-quarter combat at sub-zero temperatures, they cleared the post of Pakistani defenders.

Kargil War · 1999
Param Vir Chakra
Posthumous

Capt. Vikram Batra

13 JAK RIF · Point 5140 & Point 4875 · July 7, 1999

"Yeh Dil Maange More!" — He captured crucial peaks and sacrificed himself to save a fellow officer.

Known by the code name 'Sher Shah', Capt Batra led successful assaults in Dras and Mushkoh valleys. While rescuing an injured soldier at Pt 4875, he pushed his subedar aside, saying "You have a family and children," before taking a fatal bullet to the chest.

Insights & Unheard Events

The Toll of Valor

Out of the 21 Param Vir Chakras awarded since independence, 14 were awarded posthumously. Similarly, a vast majority of Ashoka Chakras are posthumous, highlighting that the ultimate price is often paid for the highest honor.

The Medical Corps

Bravery is not limited to combat units. Medical officers like Major Laishram Jyotin Singh (Ashoka Chakra) have laid down their lives fighting terrorists to protect unarmed staff and patients, displaying immense courage.

The Designer's Grief

Savitri Khanolkar, who designed the Param Vir Chakra, drew inspiration from the sacrifice of Rishi Dadhichi. Tragically, the first-ever PVC she designed went to her own son-in-law, Major Somnath Sharma.

"Only best of friends and worst of enemies visit us."
